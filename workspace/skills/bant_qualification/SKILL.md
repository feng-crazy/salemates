# BANT Qualification

Systematically qualify prospects using Budget, Authority, Need, and Timeline criteria to focus efforts on high-potential opportunities.

## Overview

BANT is IBM's classic qualification framework that helps salespeople determine whether a prospect is worth pursuing. It ensures efficient resource allocation by identifying deals most likely to close.

## The BANT Framework

### B - Budget
Does the prospect have money allocated for this purchase?

**Key Questions**:
- "Do you have a budget set aside for this initiative?"
- "What range are you looking to invest?"
- "How does this fit into your current budget cycle?"
- "Who controls the budget for this type of purchase?"
- "Is the budget approved, or does it need to be allocated?"

**Red Flags**:
- No budget and no process to create one
- Budget far below realistic solution cost
- Budget controlled by unknown/unreachable party
- "We're just exploring" with no allocation

**Green Flags**:
- Budget allocated and approved
- Budget range aligns with solution
- Buyer has budget authority
- Budget is current fiscal year

**Tactics**:
- If no budget: "What would need to happen to prioritize this?"
- If budget unclear: Share ranges to set expectations early
- If budget too low: Explore scope adjustments or additional value

### A - Authority
Are you talking to the right person/people who can make this decision?

**Key Questions**:
- "Who else will be involved in evaluating this decision?"
- "What's your decision-making process for purchases like this?"
- "Who has final approval authority?"
- "Who else should be involved in these conversations?"
- "Have you made similar purchases before? How did that work?"

**Decision Hierarchy to Identify**:

| Role | Involvement | Action |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Economic Buyer | Final approval | Must engage |
| Technical Buyer | Specs/feasibility | Must satisfy |
| User Buyer | Day-to-day use | Must support |
| Coach | Guides you | Cultivate |

**Red Flags**:
- Contact can't name decision-makers
- Decision-maker unwilling to meet
- Multiple unknown stakeholders
- "We'll decide internally and get back to you"

**Green Flags**:
- Direct access to decision-maker
- Clear decision process shared
- Contact is champion/coach
- All stakeholders identified

**Tactics**:
- Request introduction to other stakeholders
- Offer to present to the full buying committee
- Ask what each stakeholder cares about

### N - Need
Does the prospect have a genuine, compelling need for your solution?

**Key Questions**:
- "What's driving you to look at solutions now?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this problem?"
- "How are you handling [challenge] today?"
- "What would success look like?"
- "How do you prioritize this among your other initiatives?"

**Need Dimensions to Explore**:

1. **Urgency**: Why now? What triggers timing?
2. **Impact**: What happens without a solution?
3. **Fit**: Does your solution address their specific needs?
4. **Priority**: Where does this rank among initiatives?

**Red Flags**:
- No clear pain point identified
- Low priority compared to other initiatives
- "Just looking" or "gathering information"
- Problem can be solved with existing tools

**Green Flags**:
- Clear, articulated pain point
- Active impact (lost revenue, wasted time)
- High organizational priority
- Gap between current state and desired state

**Tactics**:
- Quantify the cost of the problem
- Connect need to business objectives
- Understand consequences of inaction

### T - Timeline
When will the prospect make a decision and implement?

**Key Questions**:
- "When do you need to have a solution in place?"
- "What's driving your timeline?"
- "What are the steps between now and a decision?"
- "Are there any deadlines or events influencing timing?"
- "If you don't have this solved by [date], what happens?"

**Timeline Stages**:

| Stage | Timeline | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Urgent | <30 days | Fast-track |
| Active | 30-90 days | Nurture actively |
| Future | 90-180 days | Keep warm |
| Unknown | No timeline | Disqualify or research |

**Red Flags**:
- "Sometime next year" or "no rush"
- Missing deadlines repeatedly
- No compelling event driving timeline
- Timeline doesn't align with budget cycles

**Green Flags**:
- Specific target date
- Compelling event (contract end, growth, compliance)
- Clear evaluation milestones
- Implementation timeline defined

**Tactics**:
- Work backwards from their target date
- Identify what could accelerate timeline
- Create urgency through implication questions

## Qualification Scoring

### BANT Scorecard

Rate each dimension 0-2:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 2 | Fully qualified - strong fit |
| 1 | Partially qualified - needs work |
| 0 | Not qualified - major gap |

**Interpretation**:

| Total Score | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| 7-8 | High priority - pursue aggressively |
| 5-6 | Medium priority - develop gaps |
| 3-4 | Low priority - nurture or deprioritize |
| 0-2 | Disqualify - redirect resources |

### Example Scoring

**Prospect A - Enterprise Software Deal**:
- Budget: 2 (Approved, $250K allocated)
- Authority: 1 (Met VP, CEO not engaged yet)
- Need: 2 (Critical compliance issue, high urgency)
- Timeline: 2 (Q2 deadline, 60 days)
- **Total: 7 - High Priority**

**Prospect B - Exploratory Lead**:
- Budget: 0 (No budget, no process)
- Authority: 1 (IT manager, unsure of decision process)
- Need: 1 (Mild interest, no pain)
- Timeline: 0 ("Maybe next fiscal year")
- **Total: 2 - Disqualify**

## Qualification Questions by Stage

### Early Discovery
1. "Can you help me understand what's prompting this initiative?"
2. "What does success look like for this project?"
3. "Who else will be involved in this decision?"

### Middle Evaluation
1. "How does this compare to other priorities right now?"
2. "What budget have you allocated for this?"
3. "What's your target implementation date?"

### Late Stage
1. "What needs to happen for you to move forward?"
2. "Is there anything that could derail this?"
3. "What's your internal approval process?"

## BANT Variations

### MEDDIC (For Complex Enterprise)
- **M**etrics: Quantified business impact
- **E**conomic Buyer: Who controls budget
- **D**ecision Criteria: How they'll decide
- **D**ecision Process: Steps to purchase
- **I**dentify Pain: Core challenge
- **C**hampion: Internal advocate

### CHAMP (Need-First Approach)
- **C**hallenges: Pain points first
- **A**uthority: Decision makers
- **M**oney: Budget
- **P**rioritization: Timeline/importance

## Common Mistakes

1. **Skipping budget** - Waste time on unqualified prospects
2. **Assuming authority** - Talk to wrong person for months
3. **Weak need qualification** - Pursue low-priority deals
4. **Ignoring timeline** - Deals stall indefinitely
5. **All-or-nothing thinking** - Disqualify too quickly vs. develop

## Quick Reference

| Dimension | Key Question | Qualification Criteria |
|-----------|--------------|------------------------|
| Budget | Can they afford it? | Allocated, appropriate range |
| Authority | Can they buy it? | Decision-maker access |
| Need | Do they need it? | Clear pain, priority |
| Timeline | Will they buy now? | Specific target date |

---

**Remember**: BANT is a filter, not a checklist. Use it to focus energy on deals that can close, not just deals that might close.
