# FAB Selling

Transform product features into compelling customer benefits through the Features-Advantages-Benefits framework.

## Overview

FAB Selling bridges the gap between what your product does and why customers should care. It translates technical specifications into meaningful outcomes that resonate with buyer motivations.

## The FAB Framework

### F - Features
What your product or service IS or HAS.

**Definition**: Factual, objective characteristics of your offering.

**Examples**:
- "Our platform has real-time analytics"
- "The software integrates with Salesforce"
- "We offer 24/7 customer support"
- "The battery lasts 18 hours"
- "It includes a mobile app"

**Characteristics**:
- Fact-based and verifiable
- Cannot be debated (true/false)
- Product-centric language
- Neutral emotional impact

### A - Advantages
What the feature DOES for the customer.

**Definition**: How a feature provides functional value or capability.

**Examples**:
| Feature | Advantage |
|---------|-----------|
| Real-time analytics | "You can see data as it happens" |
| Salesforce integration | "Your CRM data syncs automatically" |
| 24/7 support | "You can get help anytime" |
| 18-hour battery | "You can work all day without charging" |
| Mobile app | "You can access the system from anywhere" |

**Characteristics**:
- Functional benefit
- Still somewhat product-focused
- Describes capability, not value
- Begins to connect to customer needs

### B - Benefits
Why the feature MATTERS to the customer.

**Definition**: The personal, emotional, or business value that fulfills customer needs or desires.

**Examples**:
| Feature | Advantage | Benefit |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Real-time analytics | See data as it happens | "You can make faster, data-driven decisions that prevent costly mistakes" |
| Salesforce integration | CRM data syncs automatically | "Your team saves 5 hours per week on manual data entry and never misses a follow-up" |
| 24/7 support | Get help anytime | "You never have to worry about downtime during critical business hours" |
| 18-hour battery | Work all day without charging | "You can focus on your work without battery anxiety, even during long meetings" |
| Mobile app | Access from anywhere | "You can close deals on the go and never miss an opportunity" |

**Characteristics**:
- Customer-centric language
- Emotional resonance
- Addresses specific needs/goals
- Quantifiable when possible

## The Translation Process

```
Feature (What it is)
    ↓
Advantage (What it does)
    ↓
Benefit (Why it matters to THEM)
```

**Transformation Questions**:
1. What does this feature enable?
2. So what? (Ask repeatedly until customer-focused)
3. How does this solve their specific problem?
4. What's the emotional or business outcome?

## Customer-Centric Framing

### The "So What?" Test

**Before**:
> "Our software has automated reporting."
> So what?
> "It generates reports automatically."
> So what?
> "You don't have to spend time creating them."
> So what?
> "You save 3 hours every week to focus on strategic work."

**After**:
> "You'll reclaim 3 hours every week from manual reporting—time you can invest in growing your business instead of paperwork."

### Benefit Statement Formula

```
"You'll [benefit], which means [deeper value], so you can [ultimate outcome]."
```

**Example**:
"You'll close deals 20% faster, which means more revenue per sales rep, so you can hit your quarterly targets with confidence."

## FAB in Action

### Scenario: Selling Project Management Software

**Feature-First (Weak)**:
> "Our platform has Gantt charts, resource allocation, and time tracking."

**FAB-Transformed (Strong)**:
> "Our platform includes Gantt charts (Feature), which visualize your entire project timeline (Advantage), so you can identify bottlenecks before they cause delays and keep every project on schedule (Benefit).

> The resource allocation feature (Feature) shows who's available in real-time (Advantage), meaning you can distribute work fairly and prevent team burnout while hitting every deadline (Benefit).

> Time tracking (Feature) automatically logs hours spent on tasks (Advantage), so you have accurate data for client billing and can make informed decisions about where to invest your team's time (Benefit)."

## By Customer Type

### Economic Buyers (Focus on ROI)
- Frame benefits around cost savings, revenue, efficiency
- Use quantifiable metrics
- Address risk reduction

**Example**: "The automation feature reduces processing time by 60%, saving your team $120,000 annually in labor costs."

### Technical Buyers (Focus on Capability)
- Emphasize advantages alongside benefits
- Address integration, security, scalability
- Speak their language but translate to outcomes

**Example**: "The API-first architecture integrates with your existing tech stack in hours, not weeks, so you can start seeing value immediately."

### End Users (Focus on Experience)
- Emphasize daily impact
- Address frustration reduction
- Focus on ease and satisfaction

**Example**: "The one-click interface eliminates the 7-step process you use today, so you can complete tasks in seconds instead of minutes."

## Common Mistakes

1. **Stopping at Advantage** - "It saves time" (advantage) vs "You can focus on strategic work" (benefit)
2. **Generic benefits** - "It makes you more productive" vs specific, relevant outcomes
3. **Feature dumping** - Listing features without translating
4. **One-size-fits-all** - Same FAB for all stakeholders
5. **Missing the "you"** - Product-focused instead of customer-focused

## FAB Quick Reference

| Level | Question | Example | Emotional Impact |
|-------|----------|---------|------------------|
| Feature | What is it? | "Cloud-based storage" | None |
| Advantage | What does it do? | "Access files from anywhere" | Low |
| Benefit | Why does it matter? | "Work from any location without worrying about lost files or version conflicts" | High |
